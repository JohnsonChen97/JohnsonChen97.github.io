The Project
‍
How might we make the DigitalMex website user experience better for new and current users?

Overview

Digital Mex, a Mexican news platform, wanted to redesign their website. A fellow classmate of mine was working with them already and I reached out to ask if I can join in. This was a difficult task as I did not speak Spanish fluently which was a bit of a problem. Their goal was to continue their outreach to an even larger audience as they are a relatively new company that wants to make moves.

The Process

Research Define Develop Deliver

Research

In order to understand what parts of the website users interacted with, we decided to use a virtual heatmap using Hotjar. This was extremely beneficial to us as we found out the average journey of a user that visited the site.

Our findings were:

1. Most of the interactions happen at the top 50% of the page
‍
2. Only some of the links in the nav bar are used commonly
‍
3. Generally, lots of unused links and functions

When creating the low fidelity mockups, the greatest struggle we had was designing around ad placements. DigitalMex wanted to include at least 8 ads in each page. This was a problem I have never dealt with before. To overcome this, me and my partner decided to move the majority of the ads to the bottom of the page. We wanted to preserve the goal of making each page less cluttered and reached an agreement that this was the ideal solution.

Develop

Every couple of weeks, we would have to consult with the head office of Digital Mex and give them updates. This was extremely important as there were design concepts and regulations we did not know of but are commonplace in Mexico.

Define

With our findings, me and my partner started thinking of ways to solve these problems and improve the website.

Before anything, we spent time looking at other news websites. This included ones from the United States, Canada and of course, Mexico. This greatly helped us find out the common aspects that create a contemporary news website.

The changes we decided to implement were:

1. Reducing the length of pages throughout the site and removing clutter to further encourage interaction

2. Make navigation more user friendly and consistent

3. Modernize the look of the website so it can compete with other news sites

Here is the information architecture we came up with. We ended up moving around a lot of the navigation menu to clear up space in the header and put emphasis on the more commonly used buttons.

Deliver

Being satisfied with the low fidelity mockup, a high fidelity version was started. While working on the high fidelity mockups, we still communicated with their office to discuss possible changes and any feedback they had for us even though the low fidelity was completed already. We still were able to implement as many of their requests as possible while making any changes we as designers saw fit. Due to these reasons, our mockups ended up turning out different from our low fidelity version.

The new website aims to improve upon their problems while also providing the visuals of a modern website. Users are able to view the most recent top stories right when they land, but also have access to other stories as they continue to scroll down. Elements such as view count, time of post, and ratings were removed as we noticed most users did not interact with them. Essentially, we made the website less overwhelming to allow users to find the stories they want to read and quickly.

Takeaways

I learned a lot as this was my first time working with people overseas on a project like this. The online meetings that happened every other week and having a timeline to work with was fresh to me. The greatest challenge for me was the language/country barrier. Given that I have a very minimal comprehension of Spanish, attempting to get my thoughts and ideas across proved to be pretty difficult, but thankfully my partner was fluent and was able to assist in these areas. There were regulations I had to learn along the way which at times really shocked me, such as the logo which had to be larger than anything else in the nav bar. Digital Mex also had some desired features like including ads in each page and page numbers.

Overall, I understood the importance of culture when it comes to design and how it could affect the outcome of the product. It was quite an experience having to cater to a whole new user base and country. I really felt that this project made me closer to achieving my goal of being able to create for everyone.
